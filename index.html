My Brat Summer:
The Peanut Butter Story

By Armi Niemela

I love peanut butter because my favorite food is breakfast food. Since the USA is a peanut butter superpower, this summer in New York is very, very brat for me.

When I visit USA, I buy peanut butter to take home to Finland. We only have a few pb brands to choose from and the best are really expensive. In the USA there are about 20 major pb brands on the market. All pb sold in Finlans is imported, whereas in USA it is mostly made domestically.

In June after settling in Brooklyn, I got acquainted with the shelves of the nearest stores. 160 meters from my summer home, St John's Natural Foods sells two pb brands. Nearest Ideal Food Basket has 13 pb brands! That's a crazy amount. Could there be a store with even more opulent selection?

My mission: Go around the stores within 1 km of my summer home and make a peanut butter map

There are six stores with 1-5 brands.

There are eight stores with an excellent pb selection: 7-13 brands. I'm not quite sure if over 10 different peanut butter brands are sold throughout all of Finland...

Food Town of Prospect Heights is the best place for me to find pb souvenirs to take back home. They have as many as 21 different brands available.

The Foodtown of Prospect Heights is the best store for this summer in two ways: they carry three times the number of peanut butter brands compared to the average store near me. Of course, this indicates that the store has a good selection overall. Also, it's just a little over 600 meters from where I live. Only four stores are closer.

In the 15 stores I checked, there are 33 different pb brands sold: Bazzini, BNutty, Biscoff, Brad's, CB's Nuts, Creamnut, Earth Balance, Essentials Everyday Peanut Butter, Field Day, JIF, Justin's, Mara Nath, Mumgry, Nutty Gourmet, Once Again, One Trick Pony, PB2, Peanut Butter & Co, Peanut Deligth, Peter Pan, Reese's, Ricards 100% Peanuts, Santa Cruz, Skippy, Smartbalance, Smuckers, Sqrl, Sweet Ellas, Teddie, Urbanmeadow, Wild Harvest, Woodstock and Wow Butter.

Seven peanut butter brands per store on average

In Prospect Heights the most common pb brands are Skippy and Once Again. They are sold in 67 percent of the stores. Other popular brands are JIF and Peanut Butter & Co.

In Helsinki, where I live in Finland, Skippy, JIF, and Reese's are available. One large store chain sells peanut butter from the brands Nuts About Nature, Amona, and Urtekram, which I haven't seen in Prospect Heights' stores.

At the beginning of summer, I discovered a new peanut butter favorite, One Trick Pony, and now my favorite has switched to Justin's (original). Both are, of course, sold in Food Town of Prospect Heights, but I could also buy Justin's in about half of the stores near me.

I have a week left in New York so there's still time to taste new treats.
